Game Developers Conference

2009 - 2011

The Game Developers Conference (GDC) is the largest annual gathering of professional video game developers, focusing on learning, inspiration, and networking. NeuroSky has exhibited at GDC for four years, three of which I planned event and logistics (booth design, equipment rental, union requirements, etc.), managed staff and coordinated public relations. NeuroSky booths were acknowledged as one of the most popular which justified the increase in the size of our booth each year. The leads collected at these conferences made up a majority of the email marketing list and led to a number of successful partnerships.

MindSet Launch, Four Seasons

2009

I organized all of the event logistics, promoted the event to key press, secured the donation of 20 laptops from Lonovo as giveaways and arranged the MC (celebrity Marc DiCarlo). In addition, I designed and commissioned the construction, and performed the assembly of the "BrainRace" - the entertainment center piece of the event.

DEMO

2011

"The Best Place to bring your plan, prototype or product to market!" At Spring DEMO we announced a new education product. I managed all of the planning, coached the CEO and VP on their speech and performed onsite press interviews as well as ongoing, realtime, onsite promotion through social media.

The Gadget Show Live

2010, 2011 (Uk)

InIn 2010, I pitched Ns Tech to the Gadget Show conference planners and NeuroSky was given free space and placed in their technology showcase area, the "Future Tech Zone". The following year, through an ongoing relationship we attended the conference again for free in the Future Tech Zone.The Gadget show aired their second episode dedicated to winning a Guinness World Record. Our technology was incorporated into the TV show, which had been filmed a few months prior to the event, winning us a Guinness Wold Record and priceless coverage for the Gadget Show Live conference.

Casa Del Mar: MindGame Mondays

2011 (los Angeles)

As a press promotion, I arranged a partnership between NeuroSky and Casa Del Mar Hotel (LA). Each Monday evening, guests can play the NeuroSky application on three demo stations at the hotel bar. I planned the press launch event, trained the hotel staff how to properly demonstrate the technology and answer questions, and performed interviews for attending press.

Stanford Cool Product Expo

SF Bay Area - 2009 - 2011

The mission of the Stanford Cool Product Expo is to generate interest in and excitement around cool products and companies in the field of manufacturing and design at Stanford and the community at large. The CPX is made possible through the efforts of the Stanford Product Design & Manufacturing Club (PDMC), the Stanford Graduate School of Business, and the Stanford School of Engineering. For the past three years we have been the largest (and only return) exhibitor at the CPX. Attendance is by invitation only. I planned the event, oversaw the staff and coordinated press. Through relationships build with CPX, I have been invited to speak to classes in the engineering school on a number of occasions.
